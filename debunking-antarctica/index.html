Antarctica: Circumnavigation and the Impossible Ice Wall

The flat earth map's fatal flaw

The most popular flat earth model places Antarctica as a giant ice wall surrounding the disc. This makes a testable prediction: the southern hemisphere should be much larger than the northern hemisphere. Flights and sea routes should take radically longer when traveling through the south.

They don't.

Direct flights and sailing routes

A flight from Sydney to Johannesburg takes approximately 11 hours and travels roughly southwest. On the flat earth map, this route would be thousands of kilometers longer than it actually is — the flight would need to curve far north via Asia and Africa.

Multiple circumnavigations of Antarctica have been completed by ship and aircraft. The distance is consistently ~22,000 km — exactly what you'd expect for a continent of Antarctica's actual size at ~70°S latitude on a sphere. On the flat earth model, this journey would be ~60,000+ km.

Antarctica Treaty and the "conspiracy"

Flat earthers claim a global conspiracy keeps people from visiting the ice wall. In reality, Antarctica hosts ~70 permanent research stations from 30+ countries. Thousands of tourists, scientists, and adventurers visit annually. Dozens of private expeditions have circumnavigated the continent.

No credible conspiracy explanation accounts for the coordinated silence of scientists from the US, Russia, China, and dozens of other nations — many of which are fierce geopolitical rivals.
