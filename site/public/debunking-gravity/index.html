Gravity: Why a Flat Earth Can't Hold Its Own Atmosphere

The atmosphere problem

A flat disc cannot gravitationally retain an atmosphere. On a sphere, gravity points toward the center of mass uniformly from all surface points. On a flat disc, gravity would point downward through the disc — meaning the atmosphere at the edges would drift off into space.

Flat earthers often claim the disc accelerates upward at 9.8 m/s² to simulate gravity. This requires:

  1. An infinite energy source
  2. Relativistic speeds within ~1 year of continuous acceleration
  3. An explanation of why gravity varies with latitude and altitude

None of these have been provided.

Gravitational variation

Gravity is not uniform across Earth's surface. It is slightly stronger at the poles (~9.832 m/s²) than at the equator (~9.780 m/s²). This is precisely what a rotating oblate spheroid predicts — the equatorial bulge places you slightly farther from the center of mass, and centrifugal force reduces the effective gravitational pull.

This variation has been measured to extraordinary precision by gravimeters and is used daily in oil exploration, geology, and civil engineering. It has no explanation in any flat earth model.

Tides

Tides are caused by the differential gravitational pull of the Moon and Sun across Earth's diameter. They follow predictable cycles governed by orbital mechanics — cycles that have been calculated centuries in advance with perfect accuracy.

On a flat earth, there is no coherent explanation for why two tidal bulges occur simultaneously on opposite sides of the Earth.
